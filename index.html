OGWO SOUP RECIPE

Ogwo evwri soup is a soup eaten in the South-Cental region of Nigeria. It is common among the Urhobo's, Isoko's and Itsekiri's.
It is traditionally served at weddings in Delta State; its absence at a wedding celebration is considered an insult to guests. It is also
served at other traditional celebrations.

RECIPE INFORMATION

  • Preparation time : It takes about 10 minutes to get everything prepared and ready to use.
  • Cooking time : The cooking takes approximately 35 minutes.
  • Number of servings : 1 person
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • Goat meat or assorted meat
  • 1 cup Palm oil
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Potash
  • Tomatoes
  • 1 cup of Garri Filter by sieving it. Mix in 3 cups of cold water and allow to sit
  • Habanero pepper-ata rodo
  • Smoked fish and Crayfish

Cooking Methods

  1. Boil and season your meat. Once they have sufficiently cooked with some stock left, add your blended tomatoes, pepper
    and your seasoning cube.
  2. Add the crayfish and smoked fish.
  3. Bring the contents of the pot to a gentle simmer for about 10 mins.
  4. Then add some of the soup on heat into the cold water soaked garri to loosen it into a very watery mixture, then add the
    watery garri mix back into the soup on heat.
  5. Stir and allow to boil for 10-15 mins. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. The soup should gradually start to thicken at this point. Keep stirring intermittently for 5-10 mins.
  7. Dissolve potash about the size of half - 3 quarters of a knor cube in about 6 Tbsp of water and add to the pot and bring to boil. The
    presence of the tomatoes, will mean it wouldn't bubble up as much.
  8. Leave it to cook and in a few minutes, you will notice that the addition of the potash has caused the prior watery stock to thicken
    a little. Take it off the heat and let it cool down a little.
  9. Stir in Palm oil into the pot. The soup will thicken and the colour will change to a pale shade of yellowish orange.
  10. Allow to cook for about 10 mins (or until the consistency is what you want), stir intermittently so it doesn't burn.
  11. Served with Usi(starch), green plantain(boiled or grilled), eba(garri) or any other swallow.

Notes:

  • If using a fish that easily scatter, add it to the potash same as if using a fresh fish.
  • Make sure the potash is grind smooth and there are no big pieces. Mix with water, allow to sit for about 5 mins and sieve. Only use the
    drained part.
  • Filter the garri with a sieve, only use the very smooth part, the part you sieved out, and discard the grainy/shaft part.
